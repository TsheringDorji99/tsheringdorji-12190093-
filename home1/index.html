EarlyBlight

What is early blight?

Early blight is a disease that affects stressed or senescing plants. The oldest foliage shows the first signs of disease. Affected leaves develop round to angular dark brown lesions with a diameter of 0.12 to 0.16 inch (3–4 mm). In lesions, concentric rings frequently develop, producing a target-board appearance. Leaves that are severely diseased become yellow and drop. A brown, corky dry rot can be seen on infected tubers.

What is late blight?
